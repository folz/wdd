What's the Web Design Decal?
It is a 14-week, two hour per week, introduction to web design. You'll learn the fundamentals of both design and development through hands-on discussions, labs, and projects. By the end of this course, you will have the knowledge and skills required to design and build stylish, modern, and interactive websites that meet established web standards. You'll also feel comfortable putting your websites online for everyone to see.
Am I eligible to take this course?
If you're a current Berkeley student, yes! We'd love to have you.
How do I enroll in Web Design Decal?
Fill out this application. We unfortunately don't have enough space for everyone, so we have an application system in place to enroll people as fairly as possible.
What's the expected workload?
This course is fairly demanding in terms of time and material. Expect to spend a couple extra hours outside of class per week working on assignments and projects. With that said, we the staff are dedicated to making sure you feel comfortable with the material and finish the course with the skills needed to design and build classy, professional websites.
Anything I should know before starting?
There are no prerequisites for this course. Any previous design or programming experience will be helpful but is not required. However, this course will be taught assuming students are comfortable interacting with existing websites via a modern browser.
What's the grading policy?
You'll be evaluated on the basis of attendance and performance on projects and quizzes. The assignments will be weighted as follows:
  • 20% Attendance
  • 10% Quizzes
  • 10% Labs
  • 25% Mini Projects
  • 25% Final Project
with one caveat: you must turn in your final project to pass the class. We'll go over the details of grading in class.

Attendance
Due to the large amount of material that we have to cover and the limited meeting opportunities we have, attendance is mandatory and absences will impact your grades unless they are excused. Absences will only be excused for illness or exam conflict. Please let a facilitator know in advance of any absences.
Who's teaching, and how do I know they know their stuff?
Your instructors this semester include Rodney Folz, product designer and frontend engineer at Facebook; Gerald Fong, frontend engineer at Twitter; and Pedro Rodriguez, web designer for the Collegiate Starleage at Berkeley. We've all had significant industry experience in the web design and development fields.
